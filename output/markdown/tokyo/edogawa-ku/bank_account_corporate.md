## If Your Application Is Rejected

Corporate account rejection is extremely common for foreign-owned companies. Banks are rarely required to give specific reasons. Common unstated reasons include:

- Company too new (under 6 months since incorporation)
- Virtual or residential office address
- No visible business activity (no website, no clients, no contracts)
- Representative director not a Japan resident
- Business description too vague or inconsistent with Articles of Incorporation
- Industry perceived as high-risk (crypto, money transfer, consulting with no clear clients)
- Insufficient paid-in capital for the stated business scope

**What to do next:**

- Ask the bank for feedback (they may give hints even if not required to)
- Strengthen your weak points and apply to a different bank
- Consider a credit union (ä¿¡ç”¨é‡‘åº«) â€” they serve local businesses and may be more flexible
- Build 3-6 months of real business activity, then reapply
- Consult with a judicial scrivener (å¸æ³•æ›¸å£«) or administrative scrivener (è¡Œæ”¿æ›¸å£«) who specializes in foreign business setup
